III. Thermal Fluctuations

Thermal Fluctuations



- Back to $\text{CD}_n$ Model

$\vec{F}_{\text{diss.}} = -b[(\vec{v}_i - \vec{v}_j).\hat{r}]\hat{r}$


- Random force: DPD Scheme

$\vec{F}_{\text{R}} = \alpha\sqrt{\frac{2b K_B T}{\Delta t}}\hat{r}$

$\alpha$: Gaussian random number

- Ratio of thermal energy to attractive energy:

$\tau = K_B T/\epsilon u^2$

- Switch on the temperature

- back to the $CD_n$ model

- Random force form DPD

- $\tau$: thermal energy in units of attractive energy

III. Thermal Fluctuations

Flow Curves


- Stress:

$\sigma_T = \frac{K_B T}{R^3}$


- Time-scale:

$t_T = R \sqrt{\frac{m}{K_B T}}$


Peclet number

$P_e=\dot\gamma t_T$

Athermal + intermediate + thermal regimes!

- Dimensional analysis: thermal stress and time scale

- Peclet number: competition between convection and diffusion

- Three distinct regimes of flow curves: athermal - intermediate - thermal

- thermal regime: repulsive glassy physics!

- we are interested in the intemediate regime
